Hardware of the Computer


Motherboard

The motherboard is the main printed circuit board. It allows communication between
the components and parts of the system. It is referred to as the "Mother" because it is
attached and connected to so many of the parts and is necessary for the computer
to function properly. Some parts the motherboard can connect to are peripherals,
interphace cards, daughter cards: sound cards, video cards, network cards, hard drives
and many more.



CPU

The CPU(centeral processing unit) is the circuitry within the computer that carries out
the instructions of a program. There are many components of the CPU, such as the
Arithmetic Logic Unit(ALU), processor registers, and the control unit. Most modern
CPUs contain an IC chip.


RAM

The RAM(random access memory) is a form of computer storage that allows data items
to be read in nearly the same time regardless of their physical locations.


Hard Drive

The hard drive is a data storage device for storing and retrieving digital information. The
primary characteristics of the hard drive are its capacity measured in terabytes(TB,
1 TB = 1,000 gigabytes) and its performance.

How are these parts connected?

all internal components are linked with the motherboard and the motherboard allows the
components to communicate with each other.


Keyboard

a set of lettered keys in a typewriter style that are connected to the computer, you can type
out words or give commands to the computer by pressing keys or holding several keys


Mouse

a device that lets the user of point and select objects on the screen when it is moved. It
typically is in an oval shape with two buttons, one on either side, and a wheel in between the
buttons that can act as a scrolling device. However, mice also come in flat pads like in laptops


Monitor

The monitor is the screen for the computer where all images, websites, etc are displayed.
In more modern computers, the monitor is displayed with a thin film transistor liquid crystal
display(TFT-LCD) or a flat panel LED display.
